Characteristics of low porosity hair...

    🍡Takes a long time to get wet, sometimes even repelling water
    🍡Looks almost exactly the same when it is wet
    🍡Can dry slowly or quickly
    🍡Does not absorb products
    🍡Is difficult to process
    🍡Less prone to tangling
    🍡Very prone to build up since products sit on top of the hair
    🍡Prone to dryness

Products for low porosity hair...

    🍡Sulphate-Free Shampoo
    🍡Hair mask (Use sparingly)
    🍡Deep Conditioner
    🍡Leave-in Spray or Hair Milk
    🍡Mousse
    🍡Light Oil/Serum - Use after hair is 100% dry

Tips...

    🍡Deep condition when needed, and use heat.
    🍡Clarify often to avoid buildup.
    🍡Be careful with protein. Low porosity hair likely needs less protein than other porosities so don't overdo it.
    🍡Avoid using heavy products with thick oils and butters which can cause build up.
    🍡Use low suds shampoo instead of co-washing which can cause buildup.

Characteristics of medium porosity hair...

    🧁Does not get wet immediately but only takes a few minutes to absorb water
    🧁Doesn't dry quickly or slowly
    🧁Still looks curly when wet but the curls are stretched out a bit
    🧁Can be prone to build up, dryness, and over moisturization

Products for medium porosity hair...

    🧁Moisturizing Shampoo
    🧁Hair mask
    🧁Moisturizing Conditioner (Contain Hydrolyzed protein)
    🧁Medium to thick water based leave-in conditioner (mixed with 10% aloe juice 10% vegetable glycerin, 20% aloe vera juice mixed with 70% water as a spritz)
    🧁Mousse / Hair cream
    🧁Hair Oil/Serum

Tips...

    🧁Deep condition regularly and use heat
    🧁Keep protein in your routine to maintain balance, but it doesn't need to be in your daily routine.
    🧁Clarify on a regular basis to avoid buildup
    🧁Use a co-wash or low poo.

Hair oil for medium porosity...

    🧁Blackseed oil
    🧁Grapeseed oil
    🧁Neem oil
    🧁Rosehip oil

Characteristics of high porosity hair...

    🍰Gets wet quickly
    🍰Hair is very stretched out when it is wet
    🍰Absorbs products easily
    🍰often struggles with volume
    🍰dries quickly or slowly
    🍰more prone to dryness
    🍰easily over moisturized
    🍰prone to tangling

Products for high porosity hair...

    🍰Hair oil (Before wash)
    🍰Shampoo
    🍰Hair mask
    🍰Protein mask (use once a month)
    🍰Conditioner
    🍰Leave-in conditioner / Hair cream
    🍰Mousse
    🍰Oil/Serum (Use when hair is 100% dry)

Tips...

    🍰Deep condition regularly, but don't overdo it.
    🍰Use a leave in conditioner
    🍰Be careful with heavy products which can weigh down your hair, especially if it is thin.
    🍰Incorporate protein regularly.
    🍰Use a cowash or low poo (low suds shampoo) and clarify regularly

Hair oil for high porosity...

    🍰Coconut oil
    🍰Olive oil
    🍰Caster oil
×

Tips...

    🍭Use a Mild Shampoo
    🍭Follow a regular hair care routine
    🍭Limit Washing (wash 2-3 times a week)
    🍭Apply conditioner only to the lengths and ends of the hair, avoiding the roots
    🍭Schedule regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and fresh.
    🍭To add volume to straight hair, consider using volumizing products or techniques like blow-drying with a round brush.
    🍭In between washes, dry shampoo can help absorb excess oil and refresh your hair without stripping it of natural oils.

Styling wavy hair...

    🍦Shampooing and Conditioning
     Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent drying out your wavy hair
     Apply conditioner from mid-length to the ends to keep your waves hydrated and smooth
    🍦Detangling
     Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your wavy hair when it's wet to prevent breakage
    🍦Drying
     Gently squeeze excess water from your hair using a microfiber towel.
     Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel as it can cause frizz.
    🍦Styling
     Apply a leave-in conditioner or a styling cream to define your waves and reduce frizz.
     Consider using a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to enhance your natural waves.
    🍦Heat Styling
     Limit the use of heat styling tools to prevent damage to your wavy hair. If you must use them, apply a heat protectant spray first.
    🍦Weekly Treatments
     Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and moisturize your wavy hair.

Tips...

    🍦Avoid Over-Washing (wash 2-3 times a week)
    🍦Avoid Brushing Dry Hair (Brush your wavy hair when it's wet or damp)
    🍦Use a Silk Pillowcase
    🍦Protect from UV Damage (use UV-protectant products when exposed to the sun.)
    🍦Keep up with regular trims every 6-8 weeks

Styling curly hair...

    🍬Pre-Wash Treatment (hair oiling with oils like coconut or argan oil 2-3 hours before wash)
    🍬Shampooing
     Use a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your curls.
     Focus on cleaning the scalp and roots while avoiding stripping natural oils.
    🍬Conditioning
     Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your curls, focusing on the mid-lengths to ends.
     Detangle gently with a wide-tooth comb while the conditioner is in your hair.
    🍬Drying
     Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel. Instead, gently squeeze out excess water and let your curls air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting.
    🍬Styling
     Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl enhancing cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.
     You can also use a gel or mousse for added hold.
    🍬Avoid Heat Styling
    🍬Night Care
     Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and frizz while you sleep.
     You can also pineapple your curls by loosely tying them on top of your head.

Tips...

    🍬Consider protective styles like braids or twists
    🍬Avoid Over-Washing (wash your hair 2-3 times a week).
    🍬Detangle your curls with a wide-tooth comb while they are wet to prevent breakage.
    🍬 Keep your curls hydrated by using products with moisturizing ingredients like shea butter or glycerin.
    🍬Use a cowash or low poo (low suds shampoo) and clarify regularly
    🍬Regular trims every 2-3 months
